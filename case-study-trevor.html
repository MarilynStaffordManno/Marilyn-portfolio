Case Study

The Trevor Project

Words that save lives—designing compassionate crisis support content for LGBTQ+ young people when every word matters most.

Role
Content Designer (Pro Bono)
Timeline
2021
Scope
Crisis Content, Inclusive Language, UX Writing
[Project Hero Image]

The Challenge

The Trevor Project provides crisis intervention and suicide prevention services to LGBTQ+ young people. When someone reaches out in crisis, the words they encounter can literally mean the difference between life and death. The digital experience needed to feel safe, welcoming, and immediate—while guiding people to the help they need.

The challenge: design content that feels warm and human in a context that's clinical by necessity. Remove barriers without removing safeguards.

"In crisis content, there's no room for ambiguity. But clarity doesn't have to mean coldness."

The Approach

I worked with The Trevor Project's clinical team to understand the nuances of crisis communication. What makes someone feel safe enough to reach out? What language inadvertently creates barriers? How do you balance urgency with calm?

Key focus areas included:

  • Rewriting entry points to feel welcoming rather than clinical
  • Developing inclusive language guidelines that respected diverse identities
  • Creating clear pathways to different levels of support
  • Designing waiting room content that maintained connection during high-volume periods

Inclusive Language

Language that feels inclusive to one person can feel exclusionary to another. We developed guidelines that centered flexibility—ways to ask about identity without making assumptions, ways to affirm without tokenizing, ways to be specific without being limiting.

[Language Guidelines Examples]

The Outcome

The updated content helped more young people feel comfortable reaching out for support. Qualitative feedback from counselors indicated that people were arriving to conversations feeling more at ease and ready to engage.

Reflection

This project reminded me why I do this work. Content design isn't just about conversion rates and task completion. Sometimes it's about making someone feel seen in their darkest moment. The stakes are different, but the principle is the same: every word is a choice, and choices have consequences.

I'm deeply grateful to The Trevor Project for trusting me with this work.
