Case Study

T-Mobile

Redefining the Un-carrier voice across digital touchpoints and product experiences—making wireless feel less like a utility and more like a relationship.

Role
Content Designer
Timeline
2022 – 2023
Scope
Content Strategy, UX Writing, Design Systems

The Challenge

T-Mobile had successfully positioned itself as the "Un-carrier"—the scrappy challenger brand that broke industry norms. But as the company grew through mergers and expanded its product offerings, the voice became fragmented. Different teams wrote differently. The personality that made T-Mobile distinctive was getting lost in the noise of a scaling organization.

The ask: create a content system that could maintain personality at scale, while giving writers clear guidance without stifling creativity.

"How do you keep a brand voice feeling human when you have hundreds of people writing for it?"

The Approach

I worked with the UX writing team to audit existing content across the digital experience—from app onboarding flows to error messages to account management. We mapped where the voice felt strong and where it drifted into generic corporate-speak.

From there, I helped develop:

  • A refreshed voice framework with clear principles and real examples
  • A component-level content library with modular copy patterns
  • Tone guidelines that helped writers flex the voice for different contexts
  • Writing workshops to onboard new team members and align existing ones

Sample Work

The Outcome

The new content system was adopted across multiple product teams and became the foundation for how T-Mobile writes digitally. Writers had clear guidance but enough flexibility to make smart decisions in context.

Reflection

This project reinforced something I believe deeply: voice guidelines only work if they're usable. The prettiest documentation in the world doesn't matter if people can't quickly find what they need. We invested heavily in making the system searchable, scannable, and integrated into the tools writers actually used.
