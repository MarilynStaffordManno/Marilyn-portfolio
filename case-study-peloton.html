Case Study

Peloton

Energizing millions with content that pushes performance—designing motivational UX that keeps people coming back to the bike.

Role
Senior Content Designer
Timeline
2023 – 2024
Scope
Motivation Design, Voice & Tone, Product Content
[Project Hero Image]

The Challenge

Peloton's brand is built on motivation. The instructors are legendary for their ability to push people through tough moments. But outside of live classes, the product experience—notifications, achievement messages, progress updates—felt flat by comparison. The app needed to carry that same energy.

The challenge: bottle the instructor magic into scalable content patterns. Make every touchpoint feel like a tiny push toward the next ride.

"Motivation isn't just about being loud. Sometimes it's about noticing someone showed up."

The Approach

I partnered with the product team to map the motivational journey—from first ride to thousandth. What do people need to hear when they're just starting? What keeps long-time members engaged? How do you celebrate without being annoying?

Key initiatives included:

  • Rewriting achievement and milestone messaging to feel personal
  • Creating streak content that acknowledged effort without inducing guilt
  • Developing "return" messaging for lapsed members that welcomed without shaming
  • Designing pre-ride content that built anticipation

Sample Work

Streak messaging is tricky. You want to celebrate consistency without making people feel bad when they miss a day. We developed a system that acknowledged breaks as normal, celebrated returns warmly, and never guilt-tripped. The goal: make people want to come back, not feel obligated to.

The Outcome

The updated content system contributed to improved engagement metrics and member retention. More importantly, member feedback shifted—people described the app as "supportive" and "encouraging" rather than "pushy."

Reflection

Working on Peloton taught me that motivation is deeply personal. What fires up one person might annoy another. The best motivational content gives people room to bring their own meaning to it. It's less about telling people how to feel and more about noticing what they've done and trusting them to feel proud of it.
