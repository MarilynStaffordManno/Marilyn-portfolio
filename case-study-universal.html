Case Study

Universal Studios

Building excitement through words that move people—literally. Designing content for theme park experiences where every moment matters.

Role
Content Designer
Timeline
2020 – 2021
Scope
Content Design, Experience Writing, App UX
[Project Hero Image]

The Challenge

Theme parks are emotional experiences. People save up, plan trips, make memories. The Universal app needed to enhance that experience—not get in the way of it. But at the time, the app content felt functional and flat. It told you where things were, but it didn't make you excited to go there.

The challenge: make utility feel magical. Help people navigate while building anticipation.

"Nobody goes to a theme park to use an app. But the right words at the right moment can make the whole day better."

The Approach

I worked with the product and experience design teams to rethink how we talked to guests throughout their journey—from planning at home to standing in line to reflecting on the drive back.

Key interventions included:

  • Rewriting ride descriptions to build anticipation, not just inform
  • Creating contextual notifications that felt helpful, not spammy
  • Designing wait time messaging that made queues feel shorter
  • Developing a voice that was excited but not exhausting

Sample Work

Wait times are a pain point at any theme park. We experimented with messaging that reframed the wait—giving people things to look for in the queue, teasing what was coming, and celebrating when they were almost there. Small shifts in language that changed how the time felt.

[Queue Experience Messaging]

The Outcome

The refreshed app content contributed to higher engagement and improved guest satisfaction scores. More importantly, qualitative feedback showed that guests felt the app enhanced their experience rather than interrupting it.

Reflection

Working on this project reminded me that content design isn't just about clarity—it's about feeling. The same information delivered with different energy creates a completely different experience. Sometimes your job is to get out of the way. Sometimes it's to add a little magic.
