Case Study

Apple

Shaping how millions understand Siri and empowering creators across Apple Music and Podcasts—writing for products where simplicity is everything.

Role
Content Designer
Timeline
2018 – 2019
Scope
Product Content, Marketing, Creator Platforms
[Project Hero Image]

The Challenge

Apple sets the bar for simplicity. Every word has to earn its place. I worked across two distinct but related challenges: helping people understand what Siri can do (without overwhelming them), and building platforms that empower musicians and podcasters to succeed on Apple's ecosystem.

The through-line: how do you communicate capability without complexity? How do you make powerful tools feel approachable?

"At Apple, you learn that the best content is the content you don't notice. It just works."

Siri

The Siri product page needed to do something tricky: showcase an ever-expanding set of capabilities without making the experience feel overwhelming or the technology feel cold. People needed to understand what Siri could do for them—not just what Siri could do in general.

I worked on:

  • Restructuring the page around use cases rather than features
  • Writing example commands that felt natural, not scripted
  • Balancing personality with utility—Siri is helpful, not gimmicky
  • Making capabilities scannable while maintaining Apple's editorial tone
[Siri Page Design]

Creator Platforms

Apple Music for Artists and Apple Podcasts for Creators serve very different audiences—musicians and podcasters—but share a common need: helping creators understand their performance and grow their audience on Apple's platforms.

These tools are data-rich, which creates a content challenge: how do you make analytics feel empowering rather than intimidating? How do you explain complex metrics without dumbing them down?

[Apple Music for Artists]
[Apple Podcasts for Creators]

The Approach

For the creator platforms, I focused on progressive disclosure—giving people the information they need at each level of engagement, from quick glances to deep dives. Key interventions included:

  • Writing contextual explanations that appeared when and where needed
  • Developing a consistent vocabulary for metrics across both platforms
  • Creating onboarding flows that oriented creators without overwhelming them
  • Designing empty states that encouraged action rather than highlighting absence
[Onboarding Flow]

The Outcome

The Siri page successfully communicated expanded capabilities while maintaining Apple's signature clarity. The creator platforms saw improved onboarding completion and engagement with analytics features.

Reflection

Working at Apple taught me discipline. When you can't hide behind jargon or extra words, you have to really understand what you're trying to say. Every sentence gets scrutinized. Every word choice matters. It's challenging in the best way—it makes you a sharper writer.

It also taught me that simplicity isn't the same as dumbing down. The goal is clarity that respects the reader's intelligence while removing unnecessary friction. That's a balance I carry into every project now.
